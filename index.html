Manifeste

Surrealism was born after war, after ruin, after the world proved itself untrustworthy. It is happening again. And like before, the reaction is fear: rigid borders, tight uniforms, forced nostalgia for a past that never existed. Fashion has always been a tool of control, a costume for power. So we weaponize it. We strip it of obedience, unravel its meanings, and break its hold. We take what is fixed and make it move. Inspired by the Surrealist upheaval of André Breton and Philippe Soupault, we approach clothing the way they approached language: Instinctively. Subversively. Irreversibly. Les Champs Magnétiques rejects convention. It is not an aesthetic. It is a reaction. A reminder that nothing, no word, no image, no garment is beyond subversion. History repeats, but we refuse to wear its costume.

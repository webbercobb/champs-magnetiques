Surrealism was born after war. After collapse. After the world proved itself untrustworthy. It is happening again.

The reaction is fear. Rigid borders. Tight uniforms. Forced nostalgia for a past that never existed.

Fashion is a tool of control. So we weaponize it. We take what is fixed and make it move. We take what is obedient and break it.

André Breton and Philippe Soupault approached language instinctively. Subversively. Irreversibly. We do the same with objects.

Les Champs Magnétiques is not an aesthetic. It is a reaction. A reminder: nothing is beyond disruption.

History repeats. But we refuse to wear its costume.
